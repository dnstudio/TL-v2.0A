# Threadline Business Rules

This document outlines the core business and clinical rules implemented across the Threadline application.

## 1. Clinical Data Unlock Logic

The application progressively unlocks workspaces based on the availability and review status of clinical data. This ensures clinicians establish sufficient context before moving to analysis and reporting phases.

### 1.1 Evidence Workspace Unlock
To unlock the **Evidence Workspace**, a "Clinical Snapshot" must be formed by meeting the following minimum data requirements (total of 6 completed artifacts):
*   **Sessions:** Minimum of 2 clinical sessions.
*   **Assessments:** Minimum of 2 completed assessments.
*   **Documents:** Minimum of 2 documents in "uploaded" or "completed" status.

If the client is marked as `ready` (e.g., historical data complete), this lock is bypassed.

### 1.2 Analysis and Report Workspace Unlock
To unlock the **Analysis** and **Report** workspaces, the clinician must complete the Evidence Review process:
*   All evidence must be explicitly reviewed and accepted/rejected (`allAccepted`).
*   Alternatively, this lock can be bypassed if the case is manually assigned a "ready/complete" status or if `isAnalysisManuallyUnlocked` is triggered.

## 2. Regulatory & Safety Controls

### 2.1 Sequential Review Requirement (RISK-006)
In the Report generation process, there is a strict sequential review requirement:
*   Users must review the evidence summaries and formulations individually.
*   This requirement must **not** be simplified to a single "confirm all" action. Each section requires independent validation to prevent automation bias and ensure clinical oversight.

### 2.2 Cross-Tab Signalling (Hazard Warnings)
*   When there are conflicts or anomalies identified in the Evidence workspace, the application uses a hazard indicator (an orange warning triangle) on the `Evidence` tab to signal unresolved clinical conflicts. 
*   This ensures anomalies are not missed when navigating outside the Evidence layer.

## 3. Data Entities

### 3.1 Sessions
*   Represent synchronous clinical encounters (interviews, therapy sessions). 
*   They serve as primary sources of behavioral observations and verbatim evidence.

### 3.2 Assessments
*   Represent structured psychometric testing (e.g., PHQ-9, GAD-7, WAIS). 
*   Findings from assessments specify the diagnostic context and severity.

### 3.3 Documents
*   Represent collateral information (e.g., discharge summaries, IEPs, historical medical records).
*   Provide external validation or historical context for current symptoms.

## 4. Evidence Tags & Confidences

*   Evidence snippets are scored by the system across Relevance, Impact, and Confidence.
*   Evidence generated by the user is distinct from system-extracted evidence, which requires verification (indicated by the `SysBadge`).
*   Evidence can carry a `hasConflict` flag if it contradicts other established clinical parameters or previous evidence. Conflicting evidence requires explicit clinician resolution.

### 4.1. Badge Scoring Logic
The system visualizes ML-derived scores (Relevance, Impact, Confidence) using categorical badges according to the following strict thresholds:
*   **High (Score ≥ 0.75):** Rendered as green/balanced. Represents highly reliable, highly relevant, or high-impact findings.
*   **Mid (0.40 ≤ Score < 0.75):** Rendered as amber/mood. Requires closer attention, often accompanied by warnings.
*   **Low (Score < 0.40):** Rendered as rose/reflection. Subject to significant uncertainty or limited relevance.
When a confidence score is `medium` or `low`, the system can present specific causes (e.g., `missing_document`, `source_conflict`, `insufficient_data`) enabling directed clinical actions to improve the confidence rating.

## 5. Evidence Taxonomy

Evidence items are categorized by `type` and `subtype` based on the data source and nature of the observation:
*   **Verbatim:** Direct quotes from sessions. Represented internally as `type: "verbatim"` and badged as `VERBATIM`.
*   **Behavioural:** Clinician-observed behaviors. Represented as `type: "behavioural"` and badged as `BEHAVIOURAL`.
*   **Extract:** Information parsed from unstructured documents. Represented as `type: "extract"` or `type: "document"`, and badged as `EXTRACT`. 
*   **Observation:** Structured parameters extracted from clinical assessments or qualitative forms. Represented as `type: "qualitative"`, `type: "observation"`, or `type: "assessment"`, and badged as `OBSERVATION`.

If the specific sub-type is unknown, the application defaults to categorizing the entity as a generic `Finding`.

## 6. Data Flow and Processing

The lifecycle of an evidence item follows a structured pipeline:
1.  **Source Origination:** Clinical data points are introduced via Sessions, Assessments, or external Documents.
2.  **Extraction & Transformation:** Unstructured sources are destructured into atomic `EvidenceItem` units (e.g., `SessionEvidence`). These items are enriched with ML scores (Confidence, Relevance, Impact), metadata (`type`, `subtype`), and linked back to their parent structure (`sourceDocumentId`, `sourceAssessmentId`, `sessionId`).
3.  **Review Workspace:** These normalized items populate the `EvidenceWorkspace`, where they undergo validation against the clinical hypothesis. Conflicts between different items are flagged.
4.  **Actionable Outputs:** Verified evidence items seamlessly populate downstream analyses such as Case Formulations and formal Clinical Reports.

## 7. Sequential Accordion Unlock in Evidence Workspace

To enforce rigorous evaluation without overwhelming the clinician, the `EvidenceWorkspace` implements a controlled layout progression governed by sequential accordion interactions.
*   **Mechanism:** When the clinician completes a primary review section (e.g., Evidence) and expands the next phase (e.g., Clinical Findings or Follow-up & Next Steps), the system automatically collapses the preceding accordions.
*   **Dependency:** The ability to uncollapse subsequent modules directly depends on the completion state of preceding evidence review requirements.
*   **Purpose:** This guarantees focus is isolated to the active clinical decision segment preventing accidental skipping of complex case formulation steps.

## 8. Evidence Decisions and Conflict Resolution

When reviewing extracted data in the Evidence Workspace, clinicians must make explicit decisions per item, particularly when dealing with AI-generated or low-confidence artifacts.
*   **Accept:** Explicitly confirms the evidence point is valid and medically accurate. Required for all items before Analysis unlocks. 
    *   *Note:* If an item has a low AI confidence or low relevance score (indicated by rose/mid badges), accepting it indicates the clinician's manual override of the AI's low confidence score.
*   **Reject / Decline:** Explicitly invalidates the finding. The system requires structured friction (e.g., providing a reason or confirming the deletion via `isRejectFrictionOpen`) to ensure items are not accidentally dismissed.
*   **Modify:** Allows the clinician to manually amend the extracted text or associated severity before inclusion in the final assessment record.
*   **Defer:** Parks the evidence item for later review, maintaining an unresolved status. This prevents progression into definitive Case Formulation until the item is explicitly actioned (Accepted or Rejected).

### 8.1 Conflict Resolution Protocol
When `hasConflict` is true, the item inherently blocks the "allAccepted" unlock criteria. Resolving a conflict usually entails examining the conflicting source data and taking one of the above explicit actions:
*   Decline the errant observation.
*   Modify the observation to represent clinical nuance not captured in the raw extract.
*   Accept the item, confirming it as an authentic clinical discrepancy that belongs in the final report.
